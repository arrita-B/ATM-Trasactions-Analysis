# ATM Transaction Analysis | Power BI

## Introduction

This dashboard analyzes ATM transaction data for a leading financial institution in the United States with branches across **California, Texas, Washington, D.C, Grorgia and Louisiana**. The analysis covers the full year of 2022 and is aimed at the ATM operations managers, and branch managers, providing insights into customer behavior and ATM usage patterns.

---

## Business Objectives

- Understand customer demographics and transaction patterns across locations
- Identify the most popular ATM services and usage trends
- Optimize ATM operations by identifying efficiency gaps
- Spot opportunities for service improvement based on customer needs

---

## Skills Showcased

- **Data Preparation & Cleaning** — Consolidated source files, handled nulls, corrected data types, and created calculated columns (age groups, time-of-day bands, utilization rate)
- **DAX Measures** — Built KPIs including average transaction amount, utilization rate, transaction frequency per customer, and segment breakdowns
- **Core Visuals** — Bar, Line, Donut, Map, and Matrix charts to communicate usage patterns and demographic breakdowns
- **Interactive Reporting** — Slicers, Bookmarks

## Key Findings

- **Withdrawals** are the most common transaction type across all locations, followed by transfers, balance inquiries, and deposits
- **California** records the highest transaction volume, peaking between **3PM and 6PM**
- The **26–35 age group** drives the most transactions, predominantly withdrawals
- **March and April** see the highest transaction volumes, though no clear seasonal pattern holds year-round
- The **average ATM utilization rate is 12.9%**, highest in Texas, followed by California and Louisiana
- Most transaction amounts fall in the **0–20K range**, with outliers identified at the upper end

---

## Recommendations

| Area | Recommendation |
|------|---------------|
| Peak hour staffing | Prioritise ATM maintenance outside 3PM–6PM window to reduce downtime during peak usage |
| Underutilised locations | Review ATMs with low utilization rates for potential redeployment or consolidation |
| 26–35 segment | Tailor digital and ATM offerings to this dominant age group |
